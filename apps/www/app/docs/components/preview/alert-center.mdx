A single, top-level entry point for one-to-many account alerts — usage limits, hard limits, billing
problems — and the upgrade CTAs that resolve them. It replaces a stack of independent window banners with
**one** severity-colored bar that surfaces the highest-priority alert inline (its message _and_ its
call-to-action), plus a compact count-and-caret trigger that expands the remaining alerts as full-width banners,
ranked by severity.

Compose it into [`AppLayout.Notice`](/layouts/app-layout#applayoutnotice), the single top-of-window
composition slot for app-wide messaging. Alerts are **authored as JSX**: each `AlertCenter.Item` composes
the normal [`Alert`](/components/feedback/alert) banner parts as children and registers its coordination
facts (`id`, `intent`) with the center, which derives the count, the top alert, and the
severity ranking from the registrations. Mount an item to show its alert; unmount it to dismiss — dismissal
is just your state flipping a condition off. The demo runs in its own framed document — choose the
count-and-caret control to expand or collapse the full-width banners, or dismiss alerts to watch the bar
re-rank.

## How it works

- **The bar surfaces the top alert inline.** `AlertCenter.Bar` renders the highest-severity item's authored
  children — icon, title, and call-to-action — directly in the strip, so the most urgent CTA is one glance
  and zero extra clicks away. Hiding _every_ CTA behind a click is exactly what makes banners fail to convert.
- **The count-and-caret control expands the full list.** When two or more alerts are active, the bar renders
  a compact control that expands `AlertCenter.Content` beneath it. The additional alerts render as compact,
  full-width one- or two-line banners, push the app layout down, and can be collapsed by the same control.
- **Content is composed; facts are registered.** Each `AlertCenter.Item` renders nothing where it's
  authored — it registers `{ id, intent }` plus its children, and the center projects those
  children into the bar or an expansion row. You get real anchors, router links, and arbitrary JSX in every
  banner, while the count, the top alert, and the ordering stay derived and deterministic.
- **Ranking is severity, then arrival.** `danger` › `warning` › `important` › `info` › `success`; within an
  intent, alerts keep the order they first appeared — items mounting together rank in authored order, later
  arrivals append after their same-intent peers, and a dismissed alert that returns resumes its original
  position.
- **It collapses when empty.** With no items mounted the bar animates out and renders nothing, mirroring
  `AppLayout.Notice`, so `Root`, `Bar`, and `Content` can all stay mounted unconditionally.
- **It announces politely.** A persistent, visually-hidden `role="status"` live region (mounted by
  `AlertCenter.Root`) announces the top alert and how many more as alerts arrive or re-rank — a live region
  must exist before its text changes to be announced reliably. Its headline is the top alert's **rendered**
  `Alert.Title` text (inline CTA anchors stripped), re-read on every commit so the announcement stays in
  lockstep with the visible copy. The bar itself claims no ARIA `banner` landmark.

## Basic usage

Author one `AlertCenter.Item` per alert, composing the `Alert` banner parts as its children. The consumer
owns dismissal (including any persistence, e.g. `sessionStorage`): compose an
`AlertCenter.DismissIconButton` whose `onClick` unmounts the item. An item without one — like a failed
payment — is simply not dismissable.

```tsx
					</Alert.Title>

			)}

```

## The bar is the single-line form

The bar shows the top alert's icon and title, so **put the call-to-action inside `Alert.Title`** — a CTA in
the description would vanish whenever its alert leads the ranking. `Alert.Description` renders only in the
expansion rows; the bar hides it (fully — it leaves the tab order and the accessibility tree, not just the
paint). Note the corollary: the **top-ranked** alert's description has no reveal path (the expansion lists
only the alerts behind the bar), so treat descriptions as nice-to-have supporting copy and keep must-read
content in the title. The same placement mechanism is yours to use: the chrome around your children carries
`data-placement="bar"` in the bar and `data-placement="list"` in the expansion, so a class like
`in-data-[placement=bar]:hidden` hides any element in the bar the way the center hides descriptions.

## Projection

`AlertCenter.Item` renders `null` where it's authored; its children render inside the `Bar`/`Content`
chrome, which lives wherever you composed those parts (typically `AppLayout.Notice`). Three consequences
worth knowing:

- **Context comes from above `Root`.** A provider wrapping an individual item does not reach its projected
  children — wrap `AlertCenter.Root` (or the whole shell) instead. Relative router links resolve against
  the route that renders the `Bar`, so prefer absolute paths.
- **Children should be render-stateless.** A re-rank remounts an item's children (the projection is keyed
  by `id` so one alert's state can never bleed into another's). Treat alert content like toast content.
- **Don't compose `Alert.Root` in children.** The center provides the banner chrome — with the item's
  `intent` — at whichever placement the item ranks into. Children are the banner's _content_: `Alert.Icon`,
  `Alert.Content`, `Alert.Title`, `Alert.Description`, `AlertCenter.DismissIconButton`.

Because items register from effects, avoid authoring them inside `Suspense` boundaries that can hide them
(hiding unregisters the alert and the bar animates out), and server-rendered HTML contains no bar — it
enters with its height animation after hydration, the same entrance every arriving alert gets by design.

## A single alert

With exactly one alert there is nothing hidden, so the bar shows it inline with its CTA and omits the
count-and-caret control.

## Custom content

Every banner is authored JSX, so anything composes: a custom icon via `Alert.Icon`'s `svg` prop, inline
`code`, or placement-aware extras using the `data-placement` styling hook.

<Example>
	<AlertCenterCustomContentExample />
</Example>

```tsx
import { Alert } from "@ngrok/mantle/alert";
import { AlertCenter } from "@ngrok/mantle/alert-center";
import { LightbulbIcon } from "@phosphor-icons/react/Lightbulb";

<AlertCenter.Root defaultOpen>
	<AlertCenter.Bar />
	<AlertCenter.Content />
	<AlertCenter.Item id="transfer-limit" intent="warning">
		<Alert.Icon />
		<Alert.Content>
			<Alert.Title>
				Approaching your data transfer limit <a href="/billing/choose-a-plan">Upgrade</a>
			</Alert.Title>
		</Alert.Content>
	</AlertCenter.Item>
	<AlertCenter.Item id="usage-tip" intent="info">
		<Alert.Icon svg={<LightbulbIcon />} />
		<Alert.Content>
			<Alert.Title>
				Tip: agent version <code>3.24</code> reconnects dropped tunnels automatically.
			</Alert.Title>
			<p className="in-data-[placement=bar]:hidden text-sm">
				Run <code>ngrok update</code> to get the latest agent.
			</p>
		</Alert.Content>
	</AlertCenter.Item>
</AlertCenter.Root>;
```

Need a fully custom bar — different chrome, your own expand affordance? That's outside `AlertCenter`'s
scope by design: compose [`Alert`](/components/feedback/alert#banners)'s banner parts (including
`Alert.ExpandButton`) directly and manage your own state.

## Composition

Compose the parts of an `AlertCenter` together to build your own. Items render `null` in place — their
children are projected into the bar or an expansion row by rank:

```text showLineNumbers=false

AlertCenter.Root
├── AlertCenter.Bar        ← the top-ranked item's children, in Alert chrome
├── AlertCenter.Content    ← every other item's children, as ranked rows
└── AlertCenter.Item (0..n, renderless)
    └── Alert.Icon / Alert.Content / Alert.Title / Alert.Description /
        AlertCenter.DismissIconButton
```

## API Reference

### AlertCenter.Root

The renderless state owner. It creates the registration store the items register into, owns the expanded
state of the inline list, and mounts the persistent `role="status"` live region. Compose its `Bar` and
`Content` inside `AppLayout.Notice`, alongside any other window-level notice.

| Prop           | Type                      | Default | Description                                                       |
| -------------- | ------------------------- | ------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `open`         | `boolean`                 | —       | Controlled expanded state of the additional-alert list.           |
| `defaultOpen`  | `boolean`                 | —       | Uncontrolled initial expanded state of the additional-alert list. |
| `onOpenChange` | `(open: boolean) => void` | —       | Called when the additional-alert list expands or collapses.       |

### AlertCenter.Bar

The always-visible, full-width strip. It renders the highest-severity item's children inside a banner
`Alert` chrome (with that item's `intent`), appends the count-and-caret expansion control when more alerts
exist **and an `AlertCenter.Content` is composed** (without one there is nothing to expand, so no control
renders), and collapses to nothing when there are no alerts. It claims no ARIA `banner` landmark. Forwards
`className`, `ref`, and other props to the underlying `Alert` element.

When the top alert changes while keyboard focus was on a control that the change removed (dismissing alert
after alert), focus is redirected to the new bar's first trailing control instead of dropping to `<body>`;
a control that survives the swap keeps focus.

### AlertCenter.Content

The inline expansion beneath the bar. It lists every alert hidden by the bar, ranked
highest-severity-first, as full-width banner rows. It renders only when additional alerts exist and
forwards standard `div` props (its `id` is reserved — it anchors the expand control's `aria-controls`).

### AlertCenter.Item

One authored alert: the coordination facts as props, the banner content as children. Renders `null` in
place and registers with the center; mount to show, unmount to dismiss. Two mounted items sharing an `id`
is an authoring error (the last registration wins).

| Prop        | Type                                                          | Default | Description                                                                                                                 |
| ----------- | ------------------------------------------------------------- | ------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `id`        | `string`                                                      | —       | Stable identity: keys the projection, pins arrival order (a returning id resumes its spot), and stamps `data-alert-id`.     |
| `intent`    | `"danger" \| "important" \| "info" \| "success" \| "warning"` | —       | The tone the alert communicates. Drives severity ranking and the banner chrome's intent.                                    |
| `className` | `string`                                                      | —       | Optional classes for this item's chrome `Alert.Root`, in both placements.                                                   |
| `children`  | `ReactNode`                                                   | —       | The banner content — `Alert` parts, not `Alert.Root`. Rendered at the Bar/Content position, not where the item is authored. |

### AlertCenter.DismissIconButton

The per-item dismiss affordance: a thin wrapper over
[`Alert.DismissIconButton`](/components/feedback/alert#alertdismissiconbutton) that defaults its `label` to
`Dismiss ${title}` — derived from the enclosing banner's **rendered** `Alert.Title` text with inline CTA
anchors stripped and re-read on every commit, so the accessible name stays in lockstep with the visible
copy — and re-centers itself in
the bar's single-line form. Compose it — or leave it out —
per alert; its presence is what makes that alert dismissable.

| Prop      | Type                | Default            | Description                                                                            |
| --------- | ------------------- | ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| `onClick` | `MouseEventHandler` | —                  | Required. Unmount the item (update your state); the center re-ranks from what remains. |
| `label`   | `string`            | `Dismiss ${title}` | Accessible name override — e.g. when the rendered title is long.                       |

### Styling hooks

The chrome `Alert.Root` around each item's children is stamped with data attributes for placement-aware
styling (e.g. Tailwind's `in-data-[placement=bar]:hidden` inside authored children):

| Attribute        | Values                                    | Description                                        |
| ---------------- | ----------------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------- |
| `data-slot`      | `alert-center-bar` \| `alert-center-item` | The bar banner vs an expansion row.                |
| `data-placement` | `bar` \| `list`                           | Where the item's children are currently rendering. |
| `data-alert-id`  | the item's `id`                           | Per-alert styling and testing hook.                |
